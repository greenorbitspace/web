Let’s be honest – where would civilisation be without toilets? Whether it’s for a quick escape, some “me-time,” or just peace and quiet, the humble toilet is often overlooked yet truly indispensable. But for 3.5 billion people worldwide, safe sanitation is a luxury they can’t afford. As we mark World Toilet Day 2024, it’s time to flush out the inequality and focus on making sanitation accessible to all – because everyone deserves a clean, secure loo.

At Green Orbit Digital, we’re all about forward-thinking solutions that don’t just reach the stars but make life better here on Earth. And while “space data” may sound galaxies away from bathroom breaks, technologies that monitor our planet from above are shedding light on critical water and sanitation issues, helping us tackle the toilet challenge.

### Toilets: A Key to Peace, Health, and Progress

World Toilet Day 2024’s theme, “Toilets – A Place for Peace,” highlights how toilets are not only spaces of comfort but powerful protectors of health, dignity, and safety. With the Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 6 target of “Safe toilets for all by 2030” looming large, the world faces a major hurdle: almost half the global population still lacks safe sanitation. For them, sanitation is often compromised by conflict, natural disasters, climate change, and sheer neglect.

**The global sanitation crisis by the numbers:**

- **3.5 billion** people without safely managed sanitation.
- **419 million** people forced into open defecation due to lack of access.
- **1,000 children** under five dying each day from unsafe water, sanitation, and hygiene issues.

The urgency for action is real. When sanitation systems are destroyed or non-existent, untreated waste enters the environment, spreading deadly diseases like cholera. Ensuring access to sanitation services is critical for public health, and, perhaps surprisingly, it also fosters peace. Safe toilets create safe communities, promote equity, and empower women and girls, who are often disproportionately affected by the lack of private sanitation facilities.

### How Space Data is Tackling the Sanitation Crisis

So, how can space data help us solve Earth’s toilet troubles? Space-based technology, often used to monitor deforestation or study weather patterns, is invaluable in mapping and managing sanitation efforts. Here’s how it’s making a difference:

1. **Tracking Water Quality**: Satellites provide essential data on water sources, helping communities plan and maintain sanitation systems. Monitoring water bodies helps authorities detect pollution early, which is crucial for the safety of shared water resources.
2. **Disaster Response and Sanitation**: In areas hit by extreme weather or conflict, space data helps agencies identify which sanitation facilities are damaged and where immediate repairs are needed. This rapid response ensures that even in crisis situations, sanitation remains a priority, preventing disease outbreaks.
3. **Climate Adaptation Planning**: With climate change increasingly impacting water and sanitation infrastructure, satellites can provide insights for building resilient systems. By understanding how rising sea levels, floods, and droughts affect sanitation infrastructure, governments and organisations can make smarter, longer-term investments in sanitation.

At Green Orbit Digital, we believe in leveraging space technology for solutions that support SDG 6. Our commitment to sustainable practices in the space industry extends to using satellite data for Earth’s most essential services, ensuring we’re contributing to a cleaner, healthier, and fairer world.

### Toilets and the SDGs: Making Progress by 2030

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) represent a global commitment to building a just, equitable world, and clean sanitation sits at the heart of these objectives. SDG 6 aims to ensure that everyone has access to clean water and sanitation by 2030 – a goal that is vital for a healthy planet and its people.

**The connections between toilets and other SDGs are striking**:

- **SDG 3 – Good Health and Well-being**: By creating a barrier between us and our waste, sanitation services are essential to prevent diseases.
- **SDG 5 – Gender Equality**: Women and girls, especially in developing regions, often face significant risks when sanitation facilities are lacking. Safe toilets reduce vulnerability to assault and ensure dignity.
- **SDG 13 – Climate Action**: As climate change disrupts water and sanitation systems, space-based monitoring allows us to address these vulnerabilities, making sanitation infrastructure more resilient.
